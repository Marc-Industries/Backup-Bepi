\section{Risk Assessment Methodology}

\subsection{Risk Matrix}
Risks are assessed on a 5$\times$5 matrix (Likelihood $\times$ Consequence):

\begin{table}[H]
\centering
\caption{Risk Scoring Criteria}
\begin{tabular}{cl}
\toprule
\rowcolor{ecsslight}
\textbf{Score} & \textbf{Likelihood} \\
\midrule
1 & Rare — unlikely to occur \\
2 & Unlikely — could occur but not expected \\
3 & Possible — might occur \\
4 & Likely — will probably occur \\
5 & Almost certain — expected to occur \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\quad
\begin{tabular}{cl}
\toprule
\rowcolor{ecsslight}
\textbf{Score} & \textbf{Consequence} \\
\midrule
1 & Negligible \\
2 & Minor \\
3 & Moderate \\
4 & Major \\
5 & Catastrophic \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{table}

Risk Level: \textbf{Critical} ($\geq$15), \textbf{High} ($\geq$9), \textbf{Medium} ($\geq$4), \textbf{Low} ($<$4).
